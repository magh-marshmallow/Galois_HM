\noindent\large{}Let $R$ be a ring with identity $1\neq0,$ let $F$ be a field, and let x be an indeterminate over F.
\color{black}
\section*{\hspace{-1cm}14.1.1\Large{} \color{black} \normalfont 
\\ 
\\(a) Show that if the field $K$ is generated over $F$ by the elements $\alpha_1,...,\alpha_n$ then an automorphism $\sigma$ of $K$ fixing $F$ is uniquely determined by $\sigma({\alpha_1}),...,\sigma(\alpha_n)$. In particular show that an automorphism fixes $K$ if and only if it fixes a set of generators
for $K$.
\\
\\
(b) Let $G \le Gal(K/F)$ be a subgroup of the Galois group of the extension $K/F$ and
suppose $\sigma_1,...,\sigma_k$ are generators for G. Show that the subfield $E/F$ is fixed by $G$ if and only if it is fixed by the generators $\sigma_1,...,\sigma_k$.}

\section*{\hspace{-1cm}14.2.11\Large{} \color{black} \normalfont Suppose $f(x) \in \mathbf{Z}[x]$ is an irreducible quartic whose splitting field has Galois group $\bold{S_4}$
over $\mathbf{Q}$ (there are many such quartics, cf. Section 6). Let $\theta$ be a root of $f(x)$ and set
$K$ = $\mathbf{Q}(\theta)$. Prove that $K$ is an extension of $\mathbf{Q}$ of degree 4 which has no proper subfields. Are there any Galois extensions of $\mathbf{Q}$ of degree 4 with no proper subfields?}
